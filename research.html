Spatiotemporal Variation and Health Risks of Particuatel Matter in Bangkok

Duration: 2020 - 2024 Funding: Chulalongkorn Univeristy

This study was conducted to examine the spatiotemporal distribution of particulate matter (PM), determine the total concentrations of trace elements bound to PM, and assess their potential health impacts through inhalation exposure. Additionally, an analysis of existing air pollution policies using a SWOT framework was carried out to guide the development of more effective control strategies.

Research components include:

  • Analyze the spatiotemporal distribution of PM10 and PM2.5 concentrations.
  • Determine the concentrations of trace elements bound to PM and investigate their potential health impacts.
  • Assess the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats of existing air pollution policies to inform the development of more effective control strategies.

Keywords: Climate, PM2.5-10, Probabilistic Risks, Trace elements, Policy

Integrated Urban Analytics and Community Resilience: Application of Remote Sensing and Machine Learning

Duration: 2022 - 2023 Funding: United Arab Emirates Univeristy

This comprehensive research project combined multiple objectives related to urban quality of life (UQoL) mapping, population density estimation, and the integration of privately owned groceries for disaster and pandemic management. The project aimed to provide holistic insights to support sustainable urban development, community resilience, and effective disaster response.

Research components include:

  • Create an urban quality of life index map for Al Ain city, UAE, using remote sensing and GIS data to inform urban planning and socioeconomic policies.
  • Develop efficient and real-time estimates of population density utilizing remote sensing imagery and GIS vector data to support urban monitoring and disaster response.
  • Investigate the potential of privately owned groceries as crucial community resources for effective and sustainable disaster and pandemic management.

Keywords: Urban Quality of Life, Population Density Estimation, Disaster Management

Assessing Threats to Historical Oases and Urban Sustainability

Duration: 2022 - 2023 Funding: United Arab Emirates Univeristy

This comprehensive research initiative combined multiple objectives related to urban quality of life mapping, historical site preservation, and building age-informed flood hazard assessment. The project aimed to provide holistic insights to support sustainable urban development, community resilience, and effective disaster response.

Research components include:

  • Create an urban quality of life index map for Al Ain city, UAE, using remote sensing and GIS data to inform urban planning and socioeconomic policies.
  • Develop a methodology to classify building age using remote sensing data and machine learning techniques.
  • Integrate building age information with other factors, such as elevation, topography, land use, and population density, to generate a detailed flood hazard map for Al Ain City.
  • Investigate the impact of land use and land cover (LULC) changes around the oases using remote sensing data from 1972 to 2022.

Keywords: Urban Sustainability, Flood Modelling, Machine Learning, Oasis Conservation

COVID-19, Face Mask, Health, and Environment Nexus

Duration: 2021 - 2023 Funding: No

This research project explored the comprehensive implications of face mask usage during the COVID-19 pandemic, examining environmental, public health, and scientific dimensions. By analyzing mask disposal, disease transmission, and potential health risks, the studies revealed that while face masks significantly reduced respiratory infections by 61%, they also introduced complex environmental challenges. The research highlighted critical policy needs, including improved mask disposal strategies and the development of eco-friendly alternatives. The project ultimately emphasized the importance of balancing disease prevention with environmental sustainability and potential long-term health considerations.

Research components include:

  • Investigate the widespread use of face masks as a public health intervention during the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Examine the environmental impact of increased face mask production and disposal
  • Analyze the effectiveness of non-pharmaceutical interventions (NPIs) in containing respiratory infections
  • Explore potential health risks associated with prolonged face mask usage
  • Assess the broader societal and research responses to mask-related challenges

Keywords: Face mask, Littering, Biblometric analysis, SARIMA forecasting, Policy

Groundwater Recharge Estimation in Thepkasattri Watershed

Duration: 2018 - 2019 Funding: Thailand International Cooperation Agency

This research project focused on investigating groundwater recharge mechanisms in the Thepkasattri sub-district, employing advanced hydrogeological techniques to understand spatial and temporal variations in groundwater resources. By integrating multiple methodological approaches including chloride mass balance, water table fluctuation methods, and electrical resistivity tomography, the study comprehensively analyzed groundwater recharge dynamics across different seasons and geological zones.

Research components include:

  • Estimate groundwater recharge rates using multiple scientific methodologies
  • Characterize spatial variations in groundwater recharge across different geographical zones
  • Understand seasonal influences on groundwater infiltration and recharge mechanisms
  • Develop conceptual hydrogeological models to represent groundwater dynamics
  • Assess the potential for sustainable groundwater resource management

Keywords: Time-lapse ERT, Probabilistic Interpolation, Groundwater Management
